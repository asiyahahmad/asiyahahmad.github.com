About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, studying knowledge graphs and methodologies for managing and analyzing large data sets. My research focuses on developing innovative solutions to enhance data integration, retrieval, and user-centric usability within various domains.

I am advised by Ashok Krishnamurthy, who specializes in high-performance computing and data analysis, and is the director of Renaissance Computing Institute (RENCI). I currently work as a graduate research assistant at RENCI.

Prior to UNC, I attended Meredith College and graduated a semester early with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science in 2019. Passionate about equity in technology, I actively work to make computer science more accessible to women. As a recipent of the AnitaB.org Advancing Inclusion Scholarship, a mentor at Pearl Hacks, and an active member of Rewriting the Code, I strive to support and uplift women pursuing careers in technology through mentorship, networking, and advocacy.

For more information on my writing, check out my Publications. To learn about my past internships and experiences, visit the Work Experience section.

Current Projects

  • Project 1: Enhancing Knowledge Graph Usability

    We focus on developing user-friendly interfaces and tools to improve the usability of knowledge graphs. We aim to create solutions that allow users to easily navigate and interact with large and complex datasets.

  • Project 2: Synthetic Data Generator for RadX COVID Patients

    We work on integrating data from diverse sources to create synthetic covid patients. This involves developing algorithms and techniques to ensure data compatibility, consistency, and accessibility.

  • Project 3: Large Language Models with Knowledge Graphs

    We explore the multidisciplionary relationships and benefits of large language models and knowledge graphs.
