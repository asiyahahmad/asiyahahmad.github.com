About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. Candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, studying knowledge graphs and methodologies for managing and analyzing large data sets. My research focuses on developing innovative solutions to enhance data integration, retrieval, and usability within various domains, including the biomedical field.

I work under Ashok Krishnamurthy, who specializes in high-performance computing and data analysis.

Throughout my academic career, I have been honored with the Hero of Innovation awards for my patents, received the Advancing Inclusion Student Scholarship from AnitaB.org in 2024, and am a member of the RewritingTheCode network for women in computer science.

Research

Current Projects

  • Project 1: Enhancing Knowledge Graph Usability

    This project focuses on developing user-friendly interfaces and tools to improve the usability of knowledge graphs. We aim to create solutions that allow users to easily navigate and interact with large and complex datasets.

  • Project 2: Integrating Heterogeneous Data Sources

    In this project, we work on integrating data from diverse sources into cohesive knowledge graphs. This involves developing algorithms and techniques to ensure data compatibility, consistency, and accessibility.

  • Project 3: Knowledge Graph Applications in Biomedicine

    This research explores the application of knowledge graphs in the biomedical field, focusing on improving data-driven decision-making and personalized medicine through enhanced data integration and analysis.
