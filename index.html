About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, studying knowledge graphs and methodologies for managing and analyzing large data sets. My research focuses on developing innovative solutions to enhance data integration, retrieval, and user-centric usability within various domains.

I am advised by Ashok Krishnamurthy, who specializes in high-performance computing and data analysis, and is the director of Renaissance Computing Institute (RENCI). I currently work as a graduate research assistant at RENCI.

Prior to UNC, I attended Meredith College and graduated a semester early with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science in 2019. For more information on my writing, check out my Publications. To learn about my past internships and experiences, visit the Work Experience section.

Current Projects

  • Project 1: Enhancing Knowledge Graph Usability

    We focus on developing user-friendly interfaces and tools to improve the usability of knowledge graphs, with a specific emphasis on drug repurposing and hypothesis generation. Our goal is to create solutions that enable users to easily navigate, explore, and interact with large and complex biomedical datasets.

  • Project 2: Human-Centered Design Techniques for Knowledge Graph Interfaces

    We explore how Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) methods can be applied to better visualize and communicate complex knowledge graph data. We investigate techniques such as progressive disclosure, visual encoding (e.g., color, shape, and size), multi-perspective views, and interactive ranking to help users make sense of connections and relationships within KGs.

  • Project 3: Large Language Models with Knowledge Graphs

    We investigate how large language models (LLMs) can be effectively combined with knowledge graphs to support richer reasoning, entity linking, and question answering. This project explores interdisciplinary approaches for enhancing the contextual understanding and interpretability of complex biomedical and scientific data.
